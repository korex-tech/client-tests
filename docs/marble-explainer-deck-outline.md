**Slide 1 — Title.** "Marble Racing × KOREX — The Next-Generation Digital Sport, and How
It Makes Money." Subtitle: a provably-fair racing game with a real ownership economy that
doubles as the front door to a licensed casino.

**Slide 2 — In one sentence.** Marble Racing is a digital sport (think horse racing meets
esports) where simulated marbles race on physics tracks, players own and trade those
marbles like real assets, and the experience funnels players toward a licensed casino. It
earns money two ways at once: the game's own economy, and gambling.

**Slide 3 — What actually happens (the basics).** A field of marbles races down a physics
track. Winning is part SKILL (each marble's attributes: speed, acceleration, stability,
agility, endurance) and part RANDOMNESS (bounces, collisions, shortcuts). Every race is
fully simulated and PROVABLY FAIR — replayable and auditable, which real-world betting
can never offer. New races run continuously.

**Slide 4 — The player journey, step by step.** Step 1: GET A MARBLE (open a discovery
pack or buy one). Step 2: RACE IT for cash prizes. Step 3: IT DEVELOPS — the more it
races, the more it grows toward its hidden potential. Step 4: OWN & TRADE — marbles
appreciate; buy, sell, and (roadmap) breed them. Step 5: GO COMPETITIVE — build a
5-marble team, climb 10 league tiers, win league prize pools.

**Slide 5 — Three kinds of player (and why each spends).** THE RACER — short-term thrill;
deposits and races. THE OWNER — assets that appreciate; buys, grows, trades for yield.
THE TEAM BOSS — status and recurring rewards; runs teams through leagues. One platform,
three motivations, three reasons to spend.

**Slide 6 — Marbles are assets that grow in value.** A marble improves the more it races,
up to its potential ceiling. Strong performers accrue a guaranteed value floor ("Career
Value Premium"), can be sold back to the platform or peer-to-peer for more, and retire
into a Hall of Fame. Roadmap: breeding — legendary marbles produce tradable "dust" that
seeds the next generation. Players aren't just playing, they're invested.

**Slide 7 — Where the money comes from, Part 1: THE IN-GAME ECONOMY.**
High-volume / low-margin (~10%): race rake, betting, power-up cards, transfer market
(5% each side), discovery boxes. Premium / 100% margin: account upgrades, cosmetics &
skins, seasonal fees, marble certification. Plus extra margin when platform-owned marbles
finish in the money. Example: 24 marbles × $10 entry = $240 pool; 10% rake = $24 to the
platform — every race, with new races every 3 minutes.

**Slide 8 — Where the money comes from, Part 2: BETTING ON THE RACES.** League races are
bettable. Betting runs on KOREX, the licensed gambling operator, fed by race results, at
10% margin. The provably-fair simulation is the killer feature: bettors can audit that a
race was fair — which no sportsbook on real-world events can claim.

**Slide 9 — Where the money comes from, Part 3: THE CASINO.** Because Marble Racing is NOT
gambling, it can be advertised far more widely and cheaply than a casino — broad, low-cost
acquisition. Those players share one KOREX account and wallet, so an eligible, verified,
adult user is one tap from the casino: the highest-margin, always-on product. Marbles
fills the funnel; the casino monetises it.

**Slide 10 — The big picture: ONE FUNNEL, THREE ENGINES.** (1) Marble Racing acquires
players cheaply because it isn't gambling. (2) The in-game economy monetises engagement —
rake, marketplace, 100%-margin premium items. (3) Betting and the casino monetise the
intent to gamble at high margin. Already built: the provably-fair race engine, tracks, and
the KOREX wallet/identity link. Next: the live betting layer and the casino. Two
economies, one audience.
